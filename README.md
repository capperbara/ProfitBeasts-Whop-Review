## Understanding Different Types of Sports Bets

  

Before diving deeper into ProfitBeasts' performance, let's quickly review the main types of sports bets they cover:

  

**Moneyline Bets**: The simplest bet type where you pick the outright winner. No point spreads involved – just pick who wins the game.

  

**Point Spread Bets**: The most common bet type in American sports betting. The favorite must win by more than the spread, while the underdog can lose by less than the spread or win outright.

  

**Over/Under Bets**: You're betting on whether the total combined score will be over or under a set number. These are popular because you don't need to pick a winner.

  

**Parlay Bets**: Multiple bets combined into one wager. All selections must win for the parlay to pay out, but the potential returns are much higher.

  

**Proposition Bets**: Specific outcomes within a game, like how many yards a player will rush for or whether a team will score first.

  

**Futures Bets**: Long-term wagers on events like championship winners or season win totals. These typically offer higher odds but tie up your money for longer periods.

  

**Live Betting**: In-game wagering where odds change in real-time as the game unfolds. This has become increasingly popular with mobile betting apps.

  

ProfitBeasts incorporates most of these bet types into their daily recommendations, with heavy emphasis on props and totals.

## Sports Betting Legality Considerations

  

Before joining any sports betting service, you need to understand the legal landscape in your area.

  

Sports betting was federally illegal in most US states under the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA) from 1992 until 2018. Only Delaware, Montana, Nevada, and Oregon were grandfathered in with existing frameworks.

  

The Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018, opening the door for states to legalize sports betting.

  

As of January 2024:

-   37 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico have operational legal sports betting
-   30 states plus D.C. and Puerto Rico allow online sports betting
-   8 states only allow betting through physical sportsbooks
-   North Carolina has legalized but operations haven't started yet

  

Since 2018, over $20 billion has been wagered on sports betting in the United States.

  

**Important**: Laws vary significantly by state, city, and local jurisdiction. Some areas that allow sports betting may still restrict certain types of bets or betting services.
